Canada-specific regulations — why they differ from US

Canadian compliance is not a subset of US compliance. Three structural differences drive the divergence.
Decision: Canada uses PIPEDA for privacy — not GDPR, not CCPA.
US uses state-level laws (CCPA in California) with no single federal privacy law for most sectors.
EU uses GDPR — broader data subject rights, stricter breach timelines.
PIPEDA applies federally. Three provinces (AB, BC, QC) have own substantially similar laws (PIPA AB, PIPA BC, Law 25 QC). When operating in those provinces: use the provincial law, not PIPEDA.
Decision: Canada uses OSFI B-10 for banking — not OCC (US), not PRA (UK).
OCC governs US nationally chartered banks only. No Canadian applicability.
OSFI B-10 governs technology and cyber risk for federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs). Federally regulated = banks, insurance, trust companies under federal charter.
Federal-provincial split — why you must check both levels:
Federal law sets the floor. Provincial law sets additional requirements.
Alberta: PIPA AB — substantially similar to PIPEDA. Use PIPA AB when operating in AB.
British Columbia: PIPA BC — same logic as AB.
Quebec: Law 25 (formerly Bill 64) — most stringent provincial privacy law in Canada. Applies to any business handling Quebec residents' data.

Protected B classification

A Canadian government data classification. Not a compliance framework — a classification level that triggers a framework.
Protected B defined:
Protected B: Canadian government classification for sensitive but not secret data.
Examples: personal financial information, employment records, law enforcement files, medical records held by federal agencies.
Does not include classified (secret / top secret) information — that is a separate stream.
When Protected B applies:
Applies to: most federal IT systems, Crown corporations handling personal data, GC-contracted vendors storing federal data.
Trigger framework: ITSG-33 (IT Security Risk Management) — the GC baseline control set for Protected B systems.
If service processes Protected B data without ITSG-33 compliance:
Trigger: Security Assessment and Authorization (SA&A) review finds missing ITSG-33 controls.
System: Security assessment fails. Deployment blocked pending remediation.
User sees: Authorization to Operate (ATO) withheld. System cannot go live.
User can: Remediate missing controls and resubmit for SA&A review.
To restore: CIO signoff required before relaunch. Timeline: 4–12 weeks depending on control gaps.
